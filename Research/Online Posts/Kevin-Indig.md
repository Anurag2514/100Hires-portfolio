## Post 1

Date: February 24, 2026

URL: https://www.searchenginejournal.com/ai-seo-is-a-change-management-problem/568103/

Topic: AI-SEO Is A Change Management Problem

Summary: AI SEO will fail at the alignment layer, not the tactics layer.

Indig argues that the shift from crawl–index–rank to RAG, citation selection, and answer synthesis is a product-level change, not another algorithm update. Teams default to tactical responses (prompt optimization, schema, citation acquisition), but long-term failure comes from organizational misalignment — unclear mandates, fragmented ownership, and mismatched success metrics — not from lack of SEO skill.

The fix is not more tools; it is structured change management, which research suggests can improve transformation success by roughly 8×.

Key Insight: A. AI SEO is structurally different from past SEO shifts
LLMs operate on different principles than classic search. The old playbook does not bend to fit. This is a search product change and a user behavior shift, requiring reeducation even for veteran SEO teams.

B. Misalignment has recognizable symptoms

Conflicting definitions of success (rankings in ChatGPT vs. brand mentions vs. citations vs. traffic recovery)
Metrics mismatch (leadership wants traffic; SEO reports influence in a zero-click world — 84% of Growth Memo readers say LLM visibility measurement feels inaccurate)
Turf fragmentation across SEO, content, brand, product, PR, and legal
Premature tactics without shared foundations (e.g., “scale AI content” without defining AI-assisted vs. AI-generated limits)
Panic-testing instead of strategic reorientation
C. Transformation failure is the statistical norm
McKinsey and BCG data show ~30% success rates for major organizational shifts. AI SEO is not an exception; assuming tactical excellence alone will work means assuming you are the outlier.

D. Change management is the multiplier
Prosci’s 25-year research: excellent vs. poor change management correlates with nearly 8× better outcomes. BCG’s six success factors (integrated strategy, leadership commitment, talent reeducation, agile governance, effective monitoring, modern tooling) can raise odds from 30% to 80%.

E. People and process dominate over tools and tactics
BCG (2024): ~70% of AI implementation hurdles are people/process; ~10% are purely technical. Kyndryl: 95% invest in AI, but only 14% feel workforce strategy is aligned.

F. SEO’s role must evolve
From channel executor to organizational translator — educating stakeholders, explaining tradeoffs, setting uncomfortable expectations (e.g., slower traffic growth), and communicating differently to ICs, managers, and executives.

Application to AI SEO: This article directly challenges the common reflex to scale AI content production as a response to click loss.

Why it matters for content teams:

“Scale AI content” is a symptom of misalignment
Indig explicitly flags scaling AI-generated content without understanding AI-assisted vs. AI-generated limits as a premature tactic. AI content production should not start until the org agrees on why content exists in the new paradigm (citation eligibility, brand mention, entity authority) — not just volume.

Content sits at a cross-functional fault line
Who owns citation-worthy content? SEO or content? Without that answer, AI production tools multiply conflicting outputs rather than coherent strategy.

The bottleneck is not generation speed
BCG’s 70/10 split applies here: the hard part is aligning editorial standards, legal/brand review, E-E-A-T expectations, and success metrics — not whether you can produce 500 AI drafts per week.

New content KPIs are required
AI content workflows need metrics beyond rankings and sessions — LLM visibility, mention quality, citation inclusion — agreed upon before production scales.

Human-in-the-loop is a change problem, not a tooling problem
AI-assisted vs. AI-generated content requires org-wide understanding of quality thresholds, review workflows, and what to stop producing (Indig’s “kill one practice” test).

Translation role applies to content ops
Content producers must explain to executives why fewer pages with higher retrieval value may outperform a high-volume AI content farm — and get buy-in before changing production models.
